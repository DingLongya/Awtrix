About AWTRIX

In 2017 I stumbled upon the famous French smart clock and found the principle quite cool. However, there was my mind on an unusual trip, that the thing is much too expensive for what it would bring as added value for me. Very strange. From this, the idea was born to build my own pixel clock.
At that time, AWTRIX based completely standalone on an ESP8266 and with components that you could get anywhere. But after almost a year the ESP had reached its limits. Because of the low resources (memory, ram etc.), it was not possible for me to add more functions. After several attempts and tinkering, the combination of host and controller was created. This decision is based on Mainly one factor: AWTRIX is therefore independent. You can install it on an existing Raspberry that already runs the Smarthome, or you can install it on a NAS that doesn't have serial ports or GPIOs.
The host is completely written in B4X, this has the advantage for me that I only need to know this one language for Android, iOS, Windows, Mac and Linux applications, on the other hand I had my first contact with programming with Visual Basic (which is very similar).  Starting with a perceived 10% programming knowledge, I have learned a lot through AWTRIX and new insights are directly implemented in AWTRIX. This is also the reason why AWTRIX is -not yet- open source. I want to recognize and correct my mistakes myself. Besides, any professional programmer would probably fold their hands over their head if they saw my code. Until all this is fixed for my feelings, it will remain my own project for the time being, where only I am allowed to tinker with 😊.

AWTRIX is a purely private project for which a lot of my free time is sacrificed. Thanks to many many user input, AWTRIX has become more powerful than the commercial product and the ideas never stop.
Thanks to the own developed hardware like mainboard or awtrix, I was able to distinguish myself from the rather modest AWTRIX clones even on the style part. Thanks to my friend and hardware developer Matthias, AWTRIX could be made accessible to even more people.
